SpatioScholar

SpatioScholar is a temporospatial scholarship and analysis platform. It allows processing and visualizing the temporal dimension along with the physicality of the space.

Combine models and datasets

SpatioScholar allows scholars to work interactively with 3D models and primary sources, by facilitating organization of primary sources in relation to space and time. In this sense, it is a visual data management system.

Share, annotate, comment

SpatioScholar makes it possible to publish projects on a web accessible shared platform. With “Shared Scholarship” features, users can annotate directly within a 3D model, invite others to contribute to or comment on projects, and facilitate discussion.

Project Summary

SpatioScholar benefits from a number of features: the juxtaposition of temporal changes to space, the organization of primary sources in relation to space, and the annotation of simulated space.

Project History

SpatioScholar has its roots in a solution to communicate research findings of a specific doctoral project. With internal funding from NJIT, it was developed into a web accessible research platform.

Project Team

SpatioScholar is jointly led by Dr. Burcak Ozludil, and Augustus Wendell, College of Architecture and Design. Project development is led by Ulysse “Bo” Thompson, Ying Wu College of Computing Sciences.
